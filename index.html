In the Heat

It’s hot and only gets hotter. The temperature creeps higher as the sweat drips and toxins flush away. Between fight-or-flight, you gradually surrender to the heat. Your blood vessels dilate, improving circulation. Endorphins are released, whilst the tension you hold melt away.

In the Cold

It’s always colder than you remember. The air leaves your lungs as you plunge in. Your nervous system resets. Adrenaline, a big get, muscles tense, and then a jolt to the jaw. With each start, your focus sharpens and breathing steadies. Endorphins and dopamine surge. Now you’re in control.
