In the Heat

It’s hot - and it's about to get hotter. The temperature creeps higher as the sweat drips and toxins flush away. Between fight-or-flight, you gradually surrender to the heat. Your blood vessels dilate, improving circulation. Endorphins are released, whilst the tension you hold melt away.

It's always colder than you remember. The air leaves your lungs as you plunge in. Your nervous system reacts. Adrenaline surges, muscles tense, and heart rate jumps. With effort, your focus sharpens and breathing steadies. Boosting endorphins, reducing inflamation, and activating brown fat (BAT).
